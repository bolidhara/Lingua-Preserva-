Our History

The journey of Lingua Preserva began with a vision—a vision to safeguard the languages that are the backbone of our cultures, histories, and identities. Our story is rooted in a deep respect for linguistic diversity and a commitment to ensuring that the languages of our ancestors do not fade into oblivion.

In the early 21st century, as the world began to experience rapid globalization, it became evident that many indigenous and lesser-known languages were at risk of disappearing forever. Communities whose languages had survived for centuries were seeing their languages being replaced by more dominant global languages, endangering their cultural identity and history. Recognizing this growing crisis, a group of linguists, language advocates, and cultural preservationists came together to establish Lingua Preserva in 2010.

Founding and Early Struggles

At its inception, Lingua Preserva faced several challenges. With limited resources and a large-scale mission, it was a daunting task to document and preserve the languages that were rapidly disappearing. The founders had a clear goal, but they needed to develop a framework that could bring together linguists, researchers, communities, and governments to address this issue.

The first years were spent building the infrastructure for the organization—developing partnerships with local communities, creating a team of experts, and establishing a foundational language database. The initial focus was on compiling data on endangered languages and beginning the process of documenting them through research and technology. Despite the challenges, the team’s determination remained unwavering.

Key Milestones in Our Journey

Over the next several years, Lingua Preserva took major steps towards achieving its goal of language preservation. Some of the key milestones include:

  • 2012: The launch of our first comprehensive language database, which included detailed documentation on over 50 endangered languages from various regions.
  • 2015: Introduction of interactive language learning modules for the public, designed to make endangered languages more accessible and easier to learn.
  • 2017: Expansion of our team, bringing in experts from diverse linguistic fields to further enrich the database and enhance preservation efforts.
  • 2020: Partnered with major universities and research institutions globally to facilitate academic studies and fieldwork on endangered languages.
  • 2022: Launched a digital platform to allow individuals worldwide to engage with and learn endangered languages, bringing global attention to these important linguistic treasures.

Growing Recognition and Global Impact

As Lingua Preserva continued to grow, the organization gained recognition in the field of language preservation and revitalization. We were invited to participate in global conferences, and our work has been cited in academic research and policy-making discussions related to cultural preservation and linguistic rights. Our efforts have led to significant collaborations with governmental bodies, non-profits, and international organizations focused on the protection of cultural heritage.

One of the most impactful moments in our history came in 2025, when Lingua Preserva’s model for language revitalization was adopted by several governments and educational institutions as part of national cultural preservation programs. This marked a significant achievement in our journey and validated the hard work and dedication of everyone involved.

Our Legacy and Continuing Mission

Today, Lingua Preserva stands as a leading organization in the field of language preservation. With a constantly growing database, a team of passionate experts, and a global network of collaborators, we continue to fight for the survival of endangered languages. Our work is far from over, and as we look to the future, we remain committed to our mission of documenting, teaching, and revitalizing the world’s most endangered and rare languages.

Our history is one of passion, perseverance, and a commitment to preserving humanity’s linguistic and cultural heritage. We invite you to join us on this journey and be part of the movement to protect the linguistic diversity that enriches our world.
