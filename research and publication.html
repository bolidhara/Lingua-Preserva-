Research and Publications

At Lingua Preserva, our commitment to preserving endangered languages is not only reflected in our actions but also in our dedication to research. Over the years, we have conducted numerous studies, collaborated with academic institutions, and contributed to the global discourse on linguistic preservation. Our research efforts focus on the documentation, revitalization, and sustainability of endangered languages across the globe.

Through our extensive research, we have published several papers, articles, and reports that delve into the challenges faced by endangered languages and provide solutions for their preservation. These publications serve as invaluable resources for linguists, language enthusiasts, and policymakers alike.

Key Research Areas

Our research spans various facets of language preservation and revitalization. The key areas we focus on include:

  • Language Documentation: Thoroughly documenting the grammar, vocabulary, and usage of endangered languages to ensure that future generations have access to these linguistic treasures.
  • Cultural Integration: Studying how language interacts with culture, and understanding the role of language in preserving traditional knowledge and practices.
  • Technological Solutions: Exploring how modern technologies like AI and digital platforms can aid in the preservation and revitalization of endangered languages.
  • Community Involvement: Engaging with indigenous communities to involve them in the process of language preservation, ensuring sustainability and cultural relevance.
  • Linguistic Diversity: Examining the impact of language loss on global linguistic diversity and the need for preserving multilingualism.
